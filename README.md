# 📢 Agentic AI Competition 

**Build Your Ideas for Real-World Impact**  
**[ICCSE 2026](http://iccse2026.crowdscience.org/) | 8-12 July 2026 | Urumqi, Xinjiang**

## 1. Competition Overview

The **[Agentic AI Competition](http://iccse2026.crowdscience.org/competition/)** is part of **[ICCSE 2026: The 9th International Conference on Crowd Science and Engineering](http://iccse2026.crowdscience.org/)**. The competition encourages students, engineers, and researchers to build agentic copilots that can assist with meaningful real-world tasks.

Unlike a single-answer chatbot challenge, this competition focuses on systems that can understand a user goal, plan steps, use appropriate tools or skills, coordinate information, and support users through a complete task workflow.

The competition now adopts a demo-based format. Teams register on TIANCHI.com, develop an agentic copilot prototype, and submit a video and PPT report. Selected teams will be invited to present their systems at the final on-site showcase and competition during ICCSE 2026.

## 2. Task Description

**Competition task:** Each team should build an agentic copilot that supports a real-world task in one of the following five domains: **Education, Health, Law/Ethics, Sustainability, and FinTech**.

Teams should identify a concrete user group and a practical problem. The copilot should help users complete a task more effectively by planning actions, asking for clarification, retrieving or organizing information, using tools, monitoring progress, and producing useful outputs.

The final system should go beyond a generic chatbot or a simple wrapper around an existing model. It should demonstrate an agentic workflow and show how the agent helps solve a real problem in a usable, novel, and cost-efficient way.

### Example Topics by Domain

The following examples illustrate possible task directions. Teams may propose other problems as long as the task is realistic, useful, and suitable for an agentic copilot.

| Domain | Example Task | Possible Copilot Assistance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Education | Personalized Study Planning Copilot | Assist students in turning course materials, deadlines, and weak topics into a weekly study plan; the copilot can diagnose gaps, recommend exercises, track progress, and adjust the plan before exams. |
| Health | Patient Follow-up and Self-management Copilot | Assist patients or caregivers in managing post-visit instructions, medication reminders, symptom logs, and follow-up questions; the copilot should encourage safe escalation to professionals when needed. |
| Law/Ethics | Policy and Compliance Review Copilot | Assist small organizations in reviewing an AI product or data-use plan against relevant policy, privacy, fairness, and transparency requirements; the copilot can identify risks and generate an action checklist. |
| Sustainability | Campus Energy-saving Action Copilot | Assist facility managers or student teams in finding energy-waste patterns, planning interventions, assigning actions, and preparing impact reports for a school or campus sustainability project. |
| FinTech | Personal Finance Decision-support Copilot | Assist young users or small businesses in organizing budgets, invoices, cash-flow risks, and financial goals; the copilot can categorize data, flag unusual spending, and produce a responsible planning summary. |

## 5. Submission Requirements

- **Eligibility:** The competition is open to students, engineers, and researchers worldwide. Teams should consist of 1-3 members. Each team should choose one primary domain from Education, Health, Law/Ethics, Sustainability, or FinTech.
- **Submission Platform:** Teams should register and submit materials through TIANCHI.com.
- **Submission Materials:** Each team should submit a demo video and a PPT report. 
- **Video Requirement:** The demo video is recommended to be no longer than 5 minutes. It should clearly demonstrate how the developed agentic copilot helps solve the selected real-world problem. The video should show the user scenario, interaction flow, agent actions, key skills or tools, and final output.
- **PPT Report Requirement:** The PPT report should clearly explain the target problem, motivation, target users, agentic workflow, skills and tools, practical usefulness, novelty, efficiency and cost-effectiveness, responsible use considerations, and potential future improvements.
- **Originality:** Teams may reference existing tools, datasets, or platforms, but the final copilot should show original design, implementation, and scenario-specific value.

## 6. Evaluation Metrics and Awards

Projects will be assessed based on the final prototype, submitted demo video, PPT report, and final presentation if invited to the on-site showcase. Suggested evaluation metrics include:

1. **Practical Usefulness:** The copilot must be genuinely useful for target users. It should reduce workload, improve decision quality, save time, increase access to expertise, or help users complete a real task more effectively.
2. **Novelty and Innovation:** The solution should be novel in its scenario, workflow, interaction design, use of agentic skills, or integration of tools.
3. **Presentation Quality:** The team should communicate the problem, solution, demo scenario, agent skills, results, limitations, and impact clearly in the video, PPT report, and final presentation.
4. **Agentic Workflow and Technical Implementation:** The system should show meaningful agentic behavior such as planning, tool use, retrieval, multi-step reasoning, progress monitoring, or adaptive decision-making.
5. **Responsible AI and Trustworthiness:** The design should consider safety, privacy, transparency, fairness, and limitations, especially in sensitive domains such as health, law, ethics, and finance.
6. **Efficiency and Cost-effectiveness:** The solution should be cost-efficient and resource-aware. It should achieve useful outcomes with relatively low development and operating costs, avoid unnecessary computation or expensive tool calls, and demonstrate an efficient workflow suitable for real-world deployment.
